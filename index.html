What I'm Reading

A few things worth your time each week.

March 8, 2026

The Case for Permanent Capital

Byrne Hobart · The Diff

The best version of private equity looks more like Berkshire than Blackstone — patient, compounding, and structurally incapable of the forced-sale problem. The 3G comparison alone is worth the read.

Why the Datacenter Bottleneck Is Atmospheric

Casey Handmer · Blog

Handmer's orbital datacenter thesis keeps getting more interesting. The core insight — that waste heat, not chips, is the binding constraint — reframes the entire infrastructure conversation.

Mimesis and the Market

Tobias Huber · Pirate Wires

A Girardian reading of speculative bubbles. The distinction between mimetic desire and genuine conviction is exactly the frame I keep coming back to in early-stage investing.

March 1, 2026

Base Rates, Bayesian Updating, and Business Quality

Michael Mauboussin & Dan Callahan · Morgan Stanley

Mauboussin at his best — a framework for updating your priors on business quality as new information arrives. Particularly useful for anyone making decisions under genuine uncertainty.

Shenzhen's Next Act

Dan Wang · Annual Letter

Wang's letters are the single best window into how China's industrial economy actually works. Essential reading for anyone with a view on US-China tech competition.
